Appreciating Black Women in Medicine

Commending the trailblazers who transmuted healthcare

Dr. Mildred Jefferson

One of the First few African-American woman to recieve a medical degree in the U.S. (1926)

Dr. Jefferson graduated from Harvard Medical school in 1951 and devoted her career to being a surgeon.

Dr. Marylin Hughes Gaston

First African-American woman to lead a public health service bureau in 1939.

Dr. Hughes Gaston had particular intrest in improving services for underserved populations.

Dr. Jane Cooke Wright

Dr. Cooke Wright was named Professor of Surgery and Associate Dean at New York Medical college becomig the First African-American woman to be named Associate dean of a nationally recognized medical institution in the U.S.

Dr. Cooke Wright worked along side her father who was also a surgeon.
